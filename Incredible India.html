INCREDIBLE INDIA

ALL ABOUT INDIA: India is a country with no boundaries. From sky-scraping Himalayas, to bottomless seas, one can find the majority of landscapes in this country. It is the pride of the Southern Asia. India has been from the very start, one of the most technological countries in the worldWhether it be modern day science or arithmetic crunching, India has been on top of all charts. India has a widely spread history too. The great cultural heritage of the country's 28 states is rich and unique in their own way.



Indian History:

India is a country with rich and variant history. From the Indus Valley Civiliation to East India Company, it always has been a comprehensively large topic. India has always had the most number of remains of the historical places like Indus Valley Civilization, Mohenjo Daro and many more. They both time back to 5500 BCE and 2600 BC respectively. Then during the 1600's the Indies started trading with the East India Company. From 1757 to 1858 the East Indian Company started ruling India. On 1858 British Raj was established in India. This was the British Crown Rule in India. But as the enslavement increased, the Indians united together to fight the British Raj. India gained it's Independence on 15 August, 1947. The Constitution of India was effective on 26 January, 1950.







Cultural Heritage:

India's culture is boundless just like the country's geography. People speak different languages, wear various clothes and eat a wide range of cuisines. From Sambar-Dosa to Kathakali, each has its own unique taste and style. Kathak, Bharatnatyam, Kathakali, Manipuri, Odissi, Chhau, Sattriya, Kuchipudi, Mohiniattam are the 9 classical dances of India originated from Nitya Shastra. Kebabs, Vada Pavs, Rogan Josh and Dhoklas are some cultrual foods of Lucknow, Mumabai, Kashmir and Gujarat respectively. Every part of India has its own tastes in foods and dances. Like Northern part of India loves to consume spicy foods whereas the Western India keeps interest in sweets. Indian culture is spotless and is surely a site to be seen.





Religion:

Religion in India is characterised by a diversity of religious beliefs and practices. The Indian subcontinent is the birthplace of four of the world's major religions; namely Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. Indian population also comprises of Muslims, Christians and Parsis. From Hinduism to Christianity, there are significant ways for worshipping God in every religion.
